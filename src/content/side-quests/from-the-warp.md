The Imperial Grand Cruiser Solaria's Wrath burst silently from the immaterium through a widening tear in the fabric of real-space. The ship floated without life or motion amongst the tranquility of the stars for a brief moment, before it was joined in unison by its sister ships The Storm of Righteousness and Tempest's Fury. Smaller vessels, yet similar in design and no less formidable. Covered from deck to hull in battlements and crenellations, intersected with vast and formidable gun batteries, these ships were no less than flying bastions — the Solaria differing only in design from its sisters by the Imperial Cathedral at its stern. Gargoyles, angels and countless other creatures of myth adorned the Cathedral walls, no less protected by the same fortifications as the rest of the ship. These dauntless cruisers were amongst the pride of the Imperium, mechanical marvels admired by all, from High Lord to serf.

They too were joined gradually by hundreds of Imperial Frigates and Barges of lesser notoriety, huddling against their larger allies like fish in a vast ocean of unseen predators. They hung in space, letting their warp reactors cool momentarily from the immense strain they had endured over their period of interstellar travel. It had been an arduous journey through the warp, and there had been numerous casualties amongst the astropaths and service crew alike. For the last five months, the cold metal walls of the crew decks had housed the Aeras 4th Mechanised Infantry Regiment. The 11,000 souls aboard the Imperial Fleet more than fancied a change in scenery.

The Solaria's prime reactor began to warm, and one by one the engines flared into life — each a tiny glimmer in the celestial majesty of space, belying the infernal, earth-shattering sound inside the engine rooms below deck. Its kin slowly followed suit, like animals waking from hibernation, each following their matriarch to their destination. Ceres II. The men and women of Aeras were soon to be granted their wish.

The floor of the guard dormitory emanated a long, slow rumble as the Tempest's engines began to fire, catching countless troopers off balance and stumbling into each other, followed by a string of colourful curses.

"Sacred Blood, you'd think they'd bloody warn us, wouldn't you", Corporal Tizal moaned as he steadied himself against the dirty railings of Dormitory 12D.

The rumbling below deck became more of a murmur as the ship found coasting speed behind the Solaria. The journey wasn't over yet — they had still to reach orbit from the real-space rupture from where they had appeared. Tizal gave a light moan at the thought of more time amongst these troop billets. The glorious Mechanicum had laboured for countless decades making these vessels, but they sure as hell didn't spend too long on the troop compartments.

"Better than warp travel, Tiz. Barof over there spent the last five months in the latrines", came a voice above Tizal's bunk. Sergeant Riccar swung his legs over the edge of the bed, dressed in a light tank and combat fatigues. He grinned a wolfish smile at Tizal, then over to Barof.

"Fuck you… Sergeant", Barof managed weakly, before mumbling a Prayer of Deliverance to himself that he wouldn't empty his stomach over the dorm floor again.
