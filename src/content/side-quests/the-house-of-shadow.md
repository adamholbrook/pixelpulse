Even in his later years, Lyron never forgot their shrill screams as they lunged for the silver warrior who had broken their foul incantations. Cultists, hooded in ragged purple robes lined with symbols that hurt his head to understand, were gathered around a luminous circle, their chanting interrupted by the intrusion.

The cultist closest to Lyron, frothing at the mouth and still attempting to babble the last words of the spell, threw himself at the warrior in wild rage, his right arm held high to bring down the half scythe he had used during the abhorrent ritual.

Lyron was ready. His hammer still held low in a strong two-handed grip from dismantling the door, he swiftly brought the head of the hammer up, and with a change in grip to bring his left hand behind the pommel for forceful effect, thrust the great weapon into the chest of the wretch who sought his end. Undoubtedly shattering ribs and pulping the organs behind, his broken body was thrown backwards into an old book cabinet of tomes long since forgotten. The second cultist, seizing his chance to strike at the exposed warrior, struck from the side with a dagger of rusted iron. A fatal mistake on his part — Lyron skillfully reversing his grip and swinging with his body for momentum. The cultist, caught unaware by the speed of his foe, was struck at the jaw by a full hammer swing. His jaw ripped away in a flurry of bone, blood and sinew; his lifeless body dropped to the floor with a sickening thud.

The last cultist, realising the futility of a careless assault, circled his enemy to gain the measure of him. Lyron regained his stance and held his hammer in a pose his masters had taught him in his youth — his hammer held high in a firm two-handed grip, ready to either smite or deflect the next move this fool would make. Yet his enemy was cautious, far more so than the two before him. Lyron noted his skin was tattooed in red with the same symbols his robe adorned, his eyes unfocused and dilated, his jaw gnawing at flesh that wasn't there.

Enough time passed with no action, and Lyron grew impatient. "You have turned down a path of no return, heretic, and I am here to purge you from this world. Submit before me, and I will make this as painless as possible."

The cultist laughed — if it could be called as such. A mixture of phlegm and blood came forth from his mouth as he spoke in clear pain. "You have *no* idea the forces we deal with here! The Realm goes far beyond the simple walls and cities you dogs cower behind. Beings of incomprehensible power seek your destruction!" The cultist broke off, laughing maniacally at the words he had uttered.

"Silence! I will not be lectured on the imagination of a fool", Lyron commanded.

The cultist's laughter changed to anger in a moment. "FOOL?! We will see who is the fool before long! My masters promise me a life beyond this one that your childish mind can barely comprehend! The Realm will be brought to heel, Lyron Maligast."

Lyron was caught off guard for the first time that day. "How do you know my name, heretic? Speak!"

"My masters know that and so much more", the hooded cultist said. "And you will know them before long! You ALL will!" — his arms held wide, his face to the heavens.

"Enough of this nonsense. I have heard enough", Lyron lied. How did this cultist know who he was? What foul circle or magic had given him that insight? He shuddered at the thought. All that mattered was the task given to him by the Order, and he would not return empty-handed, save for the idle chatter of a madman. Running forward, he brought his wrath down upon his foe, raising his hammer high before striking down, shattering the cultist's skull with ease. There was no resistance from his target — truly devoted to whatever promise his masters had offered, the hooded figure met his end without retaliation.

Spattered in blood and visceral matter, Lyron watched the symbols on the wooden floor gradually fade. Shouldering his hammer, Lyron made for the door, ready to return to the Chapel of Light to report his success. Emerging once again into the harsh and bitter winds, he looked up to the darkened sky. Was it not light when he entered? How long had he been in that den of shadow and heresy? Lyron shook off his concern and chalked it to the fatigue he felt from the cultist's predictions. The ache in his muscles told him he would sleep well tonight.

In actuality, Lyron didn't sleep at all — his dreams haunted by faceless demons and the words of the hooded figure in the rotten house the day before.
