"I didn't think you Elves liked ale!" the bartender laughed, his beard bristles lost in a sea of chins, his large bulk swaying with his own pompous behaviour as he handed over another stein of cool, dark malt. She turned her head sharply in his direction, her stark grey eyes meeting his.

"And how many of our kin have you seen in this…", the Elf gestured to the grubby, dim and ramshackle walls of the Tavern, "… establishment." Her eyes remained locked onto his, as the barkeep took a brief yet noticeable pace backwards, her gaze seemingly looking far deeper than the surface of his unkempt physical presence.

"Begging your pardon miss, er, ma'am, I meant no offence!" the barkeep muttered.

Without breaking eye contact, she undid the coin pouch on her belt, and placed a silver coin on the bartop.

"None taken", she smiled, her hard and high cheekbones betraying none of her elf-like beauty. Turning around, she leant against the bartop, her spare hand resting on her Cleric sigil fastened onto her side. She lazily looked upon the band of miscreants and strangers before her, bellowing and guffawing over their spilt ale, bragging about their latest conquests in and outside of the bedchamber.

"How have I ended up here", Niméa Lorenshield muttered to herself, before noticing the first interesting individual of the evening.

Her gaze had caught the sight of a Dwarf in the corner of the tavern. Sat alone, his eyes were closed, his stein full and untouched. He looked as if he hadn't moved in centuries, let alone changed his garb — but then perhaps that's what all Dwarfs looked like. He was powerfully set with two shimmering axes lying beside him upon a ragged cloth. Prized possessions no doubt, handed down to him by his forefathers and their forefathers before them. What was of most interest, though, were the array of cuts around his cheeks, and the sides of his grizzled head that were bereft of the hair that covered the rest of his stout body.

"What have we here then", she grinned through perfect teeth, as she slowly made her way over to the warrior in the corner.
